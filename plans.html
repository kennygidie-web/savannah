plans that grow in the land

  • .1 GrassesTall grasses (e.g., Red oat grass, Elephant grass) – food for animals. Short grasses – survive grazing and fires, regrow quickly.
  • 2. Trees (Including Medicinal Uses)Acacia trees – thorns for protection; some species used to treat fever, coughs, and skin infections. Baobab trees – fruit and leaves rich in vitamins; bark and seeds used in traditional medicine for digestive issues and fever. Mopane trees – leaves eaten by animals; bark and roots used in remedies for stomach pain and inflammation. Neem trees – leaves and bark used as antiseptic, antibacterial, and for skin diseases. Sausage trees (Kigelia africana) – bark and fruit used for skin infections and wounds. Marula trees – fruit eaten; oil from nuts used for skin healing and nutrition.
  • 3. Shrubs and BushesDrought-resistant shrubs (e.g., Grewia, Combretum) – sometimes used in traditional medicine for digestive or respiratory ailments.
  • 4. Seasonal Plants Wildflowers, legumes, and short grasses – bloom during the rainy season.
  • 5. Cultivated Crops>Maize, Millet, Sorghum, Groundnuts, Cassava – grow in savannah farmlands.
